# All-Set-74j8 Celebration Loop — How It's Animated

`public/projects/all-set-74j8/scene-1/lottie.json`, generated by
`scripts/build-all-set-74j8.mjs`. The source SVG (`assets/all-set-74j8.svg`)
is byte-identical to the earlier `all-set-n2ie.svg` (same md5), which shipped
as `public/projects/all-set/scene-1/lottie.json` — see
`allset-celebration-animation.md` for the full source-material breakdown,
parser notes, and the stroke-to-fill / Merge-Paths-intersect / no-animated-
gradient-stops lessons, all reused verbatim here. This doc only covers what's
*different*: a materially different choreography brief drove a different rig
even though the geometry and helper functions are the same lineage.

## Same source, different physics — read the brief's frame semantics first

The earlier scene's body rig treated the beat-boundary frames (`CENTERS`,
0/54/108/162/216) as the tall/upright pose and the beat-midpoint frames
(`MIDS`) as the squashed/leaning/low pose, with no real vertical lift at all
— a bob, not a jump. This brief explicitly asked for "springing up on each
beat" with "stretching tall at the peak" and "ending down, upright and
centered" — i.e. the *landing* is squashed+upright+low, and the *airborne
peak* is stretched+leaning+high. That's the semantic reverse of which named
constant gets which pose. **Don't assume a prior scene's `CENTERS`/`MIDS`
role mapping carries over just because the beat grid math is identical** —
re-derive which named frames are "on the ground" vs "in the air" from the
brief's own language every time.

Once landing = `CENTERS` and peak = `MIDS` was settled, the real jump lift
became simple: position at `CENTERS` is the native SVG rest coordinate
(`PIVOT`, unmodified — this also guarantees the loop closes on the exact
source pose), position at `MIDS` is `PIVOT.y - LIFT` (12px up). Recipe
guidance ("add vertical lift on top of the rotation for a real jump," in
`recipe-character-rig.md`) was the reminder to add this — the lever-arm
rotation alone only bobs.

## Asymmetric per-phase easing, not a mirrored curve

`motion-taste.md`'s "ease out on the up phase, ease in on the down phase"
guidance was applied literally by giving the **landing** keyframe an
`entrance-sharp` ease (fast push-off, soft arrival at the hang) and the
**peak** keyframe an `exit-accelerate` ease (slow start from the hang, fast
fall into the landing) — on position *and* rotation. Since this `anim()`
helper stores one full segment's bezier on its start keyframe, assigning a
different `ease` per keyframe (rather than one shared default) is all it
takes to make the rise and fall feel like two different physical phases
instead of a symmetric wobble. Scale (squash/stretch) stayed on `settleSoft`
both ways — the brief's "no sharp corners" note reads better as a smooth
cross-fade of shape than as another asymmetric bounce, and stacking a third
distinct easing character onto the same beat started to feel busy in an
early pass.

## The head strokes changed jobs — read every element's *behavior* spec fresh

The two curved strokes above the head (`Vector 935` in the source) were the
earlier scene's 4th round-robin sparkle voice. This brief gives them a
completely different, explicit job: "air-movement highlights... invisible
while airborne, puff in the instant he lands... four times per loop... no
drifting or floating." That's a landing-synced impact accent, not a sparkle.
**A shape's role in a previous scene using the same source SVG is not binding
— re-read what this brief says that specific shape should do.** The pulse
anatomy (pop-hold-fade, `p == a` pivot at its own bbox center, unparented so
it doesn't inherit body position) is the same reusable idiom either way; only
*when* it fires and *why* changed.

Timed to the four landings (`CENTERS[0..3]`, with the fourth landing shared
with the loop's frame-0 wrap): opacity/scale rise into a "pop" ending exactly
on the landing frame, hold ~8f, fade over ~12f with a slight scale expansion
(100→108%) as "he springs back up," then stay at zero until 3 frames before
the next landing. The wraparound case (landing at `t=0`) is handled by
placing its pre-landing "invisible, about-to-pop" keyframe at `FRAMES - 3`
(213) and its actual pop value directly at `t=0` *and* mirrored at
`t=FRAMES` — same loop-closing trick as everywhere else in this rig, just
applied to a cyclic sub-schedule instead of the top-level beat grid.

## Losing a sparkle voice to a new job means inventing its replacement

The brief separately asks for "three sparkle bursts... plus one small
companion spark/accent trail paired with the smallest of the three." The
earlier scene got this 4th voice for free from `Vector 935` — which this
brief has now assigned elsewhere (see above). There's no unused shape left
in the source SVG for it. Rather than stretch the interpretation to force an
existing shape into double duty, a tiny synthetic mark (`sparkleTrail`, a
hand-authored 14px "+") was added near the smallest sparkle's cluster and
given the *exact same* round-robin schedule (not a delayed one — "paired
with" read more naturally as firing together than as trailing behind), just
a gentler overshoot/fade target. **When a brief asks for a decorative
accent that has no corresponding source geometry, a small hand-authored
primitive reusing the existing pulse/pivot idiom is cheaper and more
predictable than trying to carve a sub-voice out of a shape that's already
spoken for.**

## Ribbon-only sweep — tick and dot get literally nothing

The earlier scene gave the whole ribbon+tick+dot doodle group a shared
opacity breath *and* ran the light-sweep across both the ribbon and the
tick. This brief tightens that: "no... per-piece opacity motion of any kind"
on the ribbon, and "the scribbled checkmark tick and the spark dot stay
fully static, no animation at all." So the opacity breath was dropped
entirely (the doodle layer's opacity is now a flat, non-animated 100), and
`sweepGroups()` is called only on `ribbonFill.tubeSeg` — the tick and dot sit
in their own static groups in the same layer, untouched by the Merge-Paths-
intersect sweep clip. Because the sweep clips against the ribbon's actual
polygon (not a bounding box), this isolation is automatic: even though the
sweep band's rectangle sweeps directly over the tick's screen position
partway through the loop, the intersect only ever produces pixels where the
band overlaps *ribbon* geometry specifically, per the existing "separate
group per source shape" rule.

## Retiming reminder

Same beat math as the earlier scene (`BEAT = FPS*0.9 = 54`, `CENTERS` and
`MIDS` five/four-frame arrays, 3-voice sparkle round-robin at 12f stagger /
36f round / 6 rounds per 216f loop) — none of that needed to change. Only
the *meaning* assigned to `CENTERS` vs `MIDS`, and the shapes' individual
job assignments, differed. If retiming this scene, change `BEAT`/`FRAMES`
as before; the round-robin and landing-puff schedules re-derive automatically.
