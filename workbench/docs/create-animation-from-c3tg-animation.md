# create-animation-from-c3tg — How It's Animated

This documents how `assets/create-animation-from-c3tg.svg` (a green success
checkmark inside a hand-drawn circle, with two small scalloped clouds) became
the entry at `public/projects/create-animation-from-c3tg/scene-1/lottie.json`,
generated by `scripts/build-create-animation-from-c3tg.mjs`. Re-run the script
to regenerate.

## Same source geometry, different brief — reuse the rig, not the choreography

Diffing this SVG's path `d` data against already-shipped scenes (per
`svg-compatibility.md`'s prevention-first intake) turned up an exact match:
`scripts/build-cloudscheck.mjs` (project `cloudscheck`) ships the identical
circle/checkmark/cloud geometry. That earlier brief only asked the clouds to
fade in and drift; this brief explicitly asks them to also be "drawn
naturally." Reused the proven rig wholesale (path parser, trim/gradient/group
helpers, the circle→checkmark→clouds staging, the checkmark's reversed-vertex
left-to-right read) and changed only the one thing the new brief changed: the
cloud layers gained a trim-path draw-on. **General lesson**: an exact geometry
match doesn't mean the two scenes are interchangeable — diff the *brief*
too, and port forward only the delta.

## `m: 2` sequences unequal-length subpaths for free

Each cloud is one group holding three open subpaths: a long scalloped "bump"
(the cloud's main silhouette) and two short single-segment "dash" marks
flanking it (small puff/wind accents). Putting a single trim modifier on the
group with `m: 2` (trim each subpath individually) and animating one `e`
key from 0→100 across the whole window automatically draws the long bump for
most of the window, then flicks the two short dashes on in quick succession
right at the end — proportional to each subpath's own arc length, with zero
per-subpath timing math. Reads exactly like "draw the cloud, then its little
accent marks appear" without hand-staggering three separate trim ranges.
Paired with a synced position drift (`[0,14,0] → [0,0,0]`) over the same
window, "drift up" and "draw itself" land as one gesture, not two competing
ones.

## Slots added on top of a reused rig

The original `cloudscheck` build had no `slots`/`controls.json` — this brief
didn't ask for recolorability either, but the skill's general SVG guidance
(expose an accent-color slot) still applies to derivative scenes, not just
from-scratch ones. Added `accentColor` (bound to the checkmark stroke and the
three small green accent shapes — start-dot, end-dot, end-flourish) and
`cloudColor` (bound to both cloud strokes) as slots, plus two layer controls
(`cloud-left` position amount for float height, `start-dot` scale amount for
the badge-close pop). The circle's real multi-stop gradient stays un-slotted
and static, per the standing rule that only a flat fill/stroke color is a
valid slot target — animating or re-keying a gradient's stops renders nothing
in this Skottie build.
